Modules 1 & 2: Vectors & Operations M1 & M2

Vector Magnitude (2D & 3D)

To get magnitude, take the square root of the sum of the squares of components.

|A| = √(Ax² + Ay² + Az²)
Angle of Inclination (2D)

Measured from positive x-axis. Note quadrant signs to get correct CCW/CW direction!

θ = tan¹(Ay / Ax)
Quadrant Sign Guide:
• Quadrant I (+x, +y) | Quadrant II (-x, +y)
• Quadrant III (-x, -y) | Quadrant IV (+x, -y)
Unit Vector Representation

The unit vector u in the same direction of A is the vector itself divided by its magnitude.

u = A / |A| = (Ax/|A|)i + (Ay/|A|)j + (Az/|A|)k
Vector Dot Product (Scalar Product)
A • B = |A||B|cos(θ) = AxBx + AyBy + AzBz

• Coinciding: A • B = |A||B|
• Perpendicular: A • B = 0 (cos 90° = 0)
• Opposite: A • B = -|A||B|

Vector Cross Product (Vector Product)
A × B = (AyBz - AzBy)i + (AzBx - AxBz)j + (AxBy - AyBx)k

• Right-Hand Rule points in the direction of the resulting vector (perpendicular to both).

Equilibrant

Equal in magnitude but directly opposite in direction to the resultant vector.

Equilibrant = -Resultant

Module 3: Motion in 1D M3

Distance vs Displacement & Speed vs Velocity

• Distance is total length of path (scalar). Displacement is Δx = x_final - x_initial (vector).
• Average Speed = total distance / total time.
• Average Velocity = total displacement / elapsed time.

Uniformly Accelerated Motion (UAM) Kinematics
1. v_f = v_i + a * t
2. Δx = v_i * t + 0.5 * a * t²
3. v_f² = v_i² + 2 * a * Δx
4. Δx = ((v_i + v_f) / 2) * t
Position-Time Graph Analysis (x vs t)

• Slope equals Velocity.
• Flat horizontal line (slope = 0) = At rest (Velocity = 0).
• Positive slope = Moving forward. Negative slope = Moving backward.
• Curved line = Changing velocity (Acceleration).

Velocity-Time Graph Analysis (v vs t)

• Slope equals Acceleration.
• Area under curve equals Displacement.
• Flat horizontal line = Constant velocity (Acceleration = 0).
• Crossing the x-axis = Changing direction.

Module 4: Motion in 2D & Circular Motion M4

Projectile Motion Formulas (Angle Launch)

• Launch components: vx = v0*cos(θ) (constant), vy = v0*sin(θ) (subject to -g).
• Max Range is achieved at launch angle of 45°.

Time to Max Height: t = (v0 * sin(θ)) / g
Total Flight Time: t' = 2t = (2 * v0 * sin(θ)) / g
Maximum Height (H): H = (v0² * sin²(θ)) / (2g)
Range (R): R = (v0² * sin(2θ)) / g
Uniform Circular Motion
Frequency: f = 1 / T (Hz) | Linear speed: v = 2πrf = 2πr / T
Centripetal Acceleration: ac = v² / r
Centripetal Force: Fc = m * ac = m * v² / r

• Centripetal Force: directed towards the center.
• Centrifugal Force: inertial sensation, directed away from the center.

Modules 5 & 6: Newton's Laws & Friction M5 & M6

Newton's Second Law & Mnemonic
F = m * a | a = F / m | m = F / a
Mnemonic: W.A.N.T F.ood to identify forces on FBD:
• Weight (Fg = mg, points down)
• Applied force (Fa, direction of push/pull)
• Normal force (Fn, perpendicular to contact surface)
• Tension force (Ft, pulls along string/rope)
• Friction force (Ff = μFn, opposes motion)
Incline Plane Forces (Angle θ from ground)

• Parallel Component (tends to slide down): Wx = mg * sin(θ)
• Perpendicular Component (presses against incline): Wy = mg * cos(θ)
• Frictionless Incline: Acceleration down plane a = g * sin(θ)
• Frictionless Incline: Normal Force Fn = mg * cos(θ)
• Incline with Friction: Acceleration down plane a = g * sin(θ) - μ_k * g * cos(θ)

Atwood Machine (Two Hanging Masses, m1 > m2)
a = g * (m1 - m2) / (m1 + m2)
T = m1 * (g - a) = m2 * (g + a)
Elevator Systems (Tension and Apparent Weight)

• Decelerating Downward or Accelerating Upward: T = m(g + a) (apparent weight increases)
• Decelerating Upward or Accelerating Downward: T = m(g - a) (apparent weight decreases)

Exam Exception Notice: On certain FEU slides, the downward deceleration tension calculation is written as T = W - ma yielding 6240 N instead of 9440 N. Check the interactive reviewer to memorize this specific slide detail!

Modules 7, 8, 9: Energy, Momentum & Equilibrium M7-M9

Work & Power
W = F • d = Fd * cos(θ) (Joules)
Work-Energy Theorem: W_net = ΔKE = KE_final - KE_initial
Kinetic Energy: KE = 0.5 * m * v²
Power: P = W / t = F * v (Watts)
Momentum & Impulse
Momentum: p = m * v
Impulse: I = F * Δt = Δp = m * (v_f - v_i)
Collision Categories:
• Perfecty Elastic: Momentum & KE conserved.
• Perfecty Inelastic: Objects stick together. Final mass is (m1+m2). Momentum conserved, KE is NOT.
Equilibrium & Torque

1. Translational Equilibrium: ΣF = 0 (ΣFx = 0, ΣFy = 0)
2. Rotational Equilibrium: Στ = 0 (Στ_cw = Στ_ccw)

Torque: τ = r * F * sin(θ) (N•m)
